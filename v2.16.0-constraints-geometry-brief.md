## 2. Construction Settings vs. regs columns — RESOLVED

v2.2.0's Construction Settings tab holds global going min/max, hard width maximum, and the regs-warning toggle via `stairBuilderVars.construction`. These overlap the new per-regime columns.

**Decision (Dan, 2026-07-21): regime values override global where present. Global remains the fallback when the active regime supplies no value.**

- Preserve the existing soft-warning (red, still allowed) vs hard-max (snap-back) distinction
- Add a note under the Construction Settings fields stating they are overridden when a regime supplies a value

**The override MUST extend to hard maximums, not just minimums.** If the global going hard-max is 250 and Commercial – General Access requires a 280mm minimum going, the two are mutually unsatisfiable and the staircase becomes unbuildable. Regime minimums must be able to raise a global maximum out of the way. Verification item 6 covers exactly this.

### 3.2 Seed rows

Approved Document K distinguishes two non-domestic classifications with materially different limits. The client's supplied figures (250 going / 190 rise) are the **Utility** case. Quoting a public-circulation stair to Utility limits would be non-compliant, so the two are seeded separately.

| row | min_going | max_rise | min_rise | max_pitch | 2R+G | max_open_gap | max_risers_run | typical use |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Domestic – England & Wales | 220 | 220 | — | 42 | 550–700 | 100 | — | Dwellings |
| Commercial – General Access | 280 | 170 | 150 | 31.3 | 550–700 | 100 | 16 | Retail, offices, schools, public circulation |
| Commercial – Utility | 250 | 190 | 150 | 37.2 | 550–700 | 100 | 16 | Maintenance, secondary escape, staff-only |
| No Building Regs | — | — | — | — | — | — | — | Unregulated |

Approved Document K sets no minimum pitch; the 2R+G rule bounds shallow pitches in practice. No `min_pitch` column. Scotland is a future row, not a code change.

**Both commercial rows are customer-selectable (confirmed Dan, 2026-07-21), so the labels and descriptions carry real weight.** "General Access" and "Utility" are Approved Document K terms, not customer language, and choosing wrong moves the going minimum by 30mm and the pitch limit by nearly 6°. Populate `description` on every row and surface it under the option in the form — the "typical use" column above is the intended copy. Consider labelling them plainly, e.g. "Commercial – public areas" and "Commercial – staff & maintenance only", with the formal ADK term in the description rather than the label.

**Escalation copy** on both commercial rows: direct to the quarter/half landing calculators, or to call for advice. URLs are settings fields — do not hardcode SPD permalinks.

### 3.3 Wire `max_pitch` into `getStaircaseConfig()`

v2.10.0 hardcodes the 42° Doc K limit in the riser-config validation loop. Replace with the active regime's `max_pitch`. Fall back to 42 only when a regime is selected but supplies no value; under "No Building Regs" there is no pitch gate at all.

Keep `calculateStepPitch(riserh, going)` — the Doc K per-step definition is correct and stays.

**Regression risk:** this changes which riser counts the dropdown offers. Re-run the v2.10.0 boundary cases (2600/13/250 → 38.66°; 200/220 → 42.27° excluded; 198/220 → 41.99° included) and confirm they are unchanged under Domestic.

### 3.4 Availability — TWO mechanisms, both required

The client's rules are **restrictive** ("only X should be selectable"), not merely subtractive. A tag-to-restrict scheme alone cannot express them: an untagged row means "available to all", and "all" includes the restrictive construction type, so untagged rows leak in. Two pieces are needed.

**(a) `available_for` — multi-select on each material/profile row**

Added to `stringer_types`, `tread_types`, `riser_types`, and the Tread Profile repeater. Options populated dynamically from `construction_type.code` — do not hardcode the list.

**(b) `strict_for` — multi-select on `construction_type`**

Names which repeaters this construction type gates strictly. Values are repeater identifiers: `stringer_types`, `tread_types`, `riser_types`, `tread_profiles`.

**Resolution rule, per repeater:**

| repeater is… | behaviour |
|---|---|
| **not** in the construction type's `strict_for` | Permissive. Untagged rows available; a non-empty `available_for` restricts that row to the listed types. |
| in `strict_for` | Strict. **Only** rows whose `available_for` includes this construction type are available. Untagged rows are excluded. |

Mapped to the client's rules:

| construction type | `strict_for` | outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | — | all thicknesses and profiles available |
| Cut String | `tread_profiles` | only the 3 tagged profiles; all material thicknesses available |
| Open Riser | `tread_types`, `riser_types` | only tagged 30mm treads and open-riser boards; all nosing profiles available |

Properties worth preserving:

- **Zero migration.** Standard is permissive with nothing tagged, so existing rows behave exactly as today.
- **Adding a material** to a permissive repeater needs no thought; adding one intended for a strict repeater requires ticking the box, which is visible at the point of creation.
- **Deleting a material** removes its rule with it. No orphaned rules table.

**Do not build a separate rules screen.** Tags live on the rows; strictness lives on the construction type. Nothing maps type → list of allowed IDs in a third place.

### 3.5 `construction_type` — other new sub-fields

| `derives_riser_height` | checkbox | Riser board height computed for display rather than fixed (§4). |

## 4. Open riser

Open riser **substitutes** a shorter riser board; it does not remove the riser component.

### 4.1 New material rows

The open-riser boards are **client products with client prices**. Per §11, **ship zero rows.** Do not seed placeholder rows, and specifically do not seed a row priced at £0.00 — a selectable row with a zero price quotes silently and wrongly, which is worse than no row at all.

Because `riser_types` is in Open Riser's `strict_for`, zero tagged rows means the strict filter returns nothing, and §6.1 already specifies the correct response: warn in admin and do not offer the construction type. **Open Riser is therefore unavailable until the client configures their own boards** — self-documenting, and impossible to mis-quote.

When the client does configure them (e.g. "Open Riser Board – Pine" / "– Oak"), each carries a **flat unit price** and is tagged `available_for: ["open_riser"]`. Standard 18mm rows are excluded automatically — **no existing row needs editing.**

Likewise tag the 30mm solid pine and oak `tread_types` rows with `available_for: ["open_riser"]`. Tread profiles stay untagged: all nosing profiles remain available.

### 4.2 Derived board height — display only

```
gap    = regime.max_open_gap ?: construction_type.default_open_gap
height = individual_rise - gap
```

At 186mm rise with a 100mm gap: 86mm board.

**The fallback is required, not defensive.** Under "No Building Regs" the regime supplies no `max_open_gap`, but the board still physically exists. Height must never resolve to null or zero.

**Price is flat and does not track board height.** The derived height is spec information for the panel and PDF only. No new pricing mechanism.

### 4.3 Implementation checks

- Confirm `priceCalc.js` applies the riser line correctly under open riser — pre-production, so fix directly if wrong.
- **No canvas work required.** The 2D diagram is a top-down plan view; riser faces are never drawn, so an open-riser flight is visually identical to a closed one in plan. Confirmed by Dan, 2026-07-21.

## 5. Top lip — 43mm, additive, DISPLAY ONLY

Fixed 43mm component at the head of the flight, before connection to the floor above. Not a nosing. Does not vary by tread profile or construction type — **it is always present**. **Confirmed already absorbed in current prices — do not add a price field.**

On turned staircases the lip occurs **once**, where the staircase meets the upper floor — not at each intermediate landing, since intermediate flights terminate at a landing rather than a floor. Confirmed by Dan, 2026-07-21.

For half and quarter turns this means the lip is added to the **final flight only** when composing the plan footprint. Do not add it per flight.

```
display_total_run = (tread_count * going) + top_lip_mm
```

Reference config (14 risers / 13 treads / 240 going): 3120 → **3163mm**.

### 5.1 Apply to

- Measurements panel total run and overall plan length
- Minimum stairwell opening
- **The canvas — drawn geometry, not just the dimension label**

The 2D diagram is a **top-down plan view**, so the lip is physically visible in it: 43mm of extra depth at the head of the flight, beyond the last tread. Changing the dimension line to 3163mm without drawing that 43mm leaves the label contradicting the drawing — 13 treads × 240mm still measures 3120mm on screen.

Draw the lip as a thin element at the head of the flight in `Stairs.js`, then run the dimension line over the full 3163mm. Same commit as the panel change, no exceptions. A panel reading 3163 beside a drawing that measures 3120 destroys the credibility the panel exists to build.

### 5.2 Do NOT apply to pricing geometry

`total_run` is computed in `grabFormValues` (v2.12.1) and is **already consumed by two pricing paths**:

1. **Metal spindle count** (v2.4.0): `ceil(stairRun / 141) + ceil(landingRun / 112)`, deliberately matched to the live `baltic-wc-spindle-calculator` product page under the 240mm-going / 42° rake assumption.
2. **`calculateRake()`** drives material lengths (the v2.10.0 audit notes the generous hypotenuse is intentional).

Adding 43mm of run adds roughly 54mm of rake at 37.78°, tipping the metal count by one on some configurations and putting the configurator out of step with the live calculator.

**This is the one boundary the pre-production status does not relax** — `baltic-wc-spindle-calculator` *is* live, and customers compare the two.

**Implementation requirement:** introduce a **separate** `display_total_run`. Leave `total_run` byte-identical. Inline comment at both the definition and the point of use stating the boundary and why.

## 6. Enforcement — three layers

### 6.1 Render / filter (JS)

Localise the regs table, the `available_for` map and each construction type's `strict_for` alongside the existing `stairBuilderVars.construction` payload.

On construction-type change, filter the `<option>` elements per the §3.4 resolution rule.

- **Filter individual options — do not disable the select.** Disabled selects don't POST.
- **Do not CSS-hide options.** CSS-hidden inputs still POST.
- If filtering leaves exactly one option, reuse `bd_stairbuilder_render_type_field()`'s hidden-input collapse.
- **If a strict repeater filters to zero options**, that is a misconfiguration, not a valid state. Show an admin-facing warning and leave the previous selection rather than silently emptying the select.

### 6.2 Invalid-state fallback

When a construction change invalidates the current selection:

1. Fall back to the first valid option
2. Show a notice using the existing `.sb-limit-msg` style
3. **Fire the change event** — `priceCalc.js` reads prices off the injected `<option value="pine:PRICE">` markup and won't recalculate without it

Step 2 is not optional. A price that silently changes underneath the user destroys trust in the number, which is the product.

### 6.3 Server-side revalidation

Price is client-side, so a lead POST can carry an impossible combination. Re-validate against the same settings tables — including `strict_for` — before writing the lead or generating the PDF. Reject clearly; do not silently correct and quote.

### 6.4 Escalation

`block` mode reuses `window.bdFlightInvalid` from v2.11.0 — priceCalc already blanks outputs on that flag. Do not invent a second path.

Both commercial rows cap at 16 risers in a continuous run. Confirmed intended: at 190mm max rise this tops out around 3040mm floor-to-floor, and at 170mm around 2720mm, so it fires regularly on commercial straight flights. That is desired — it routes the customer to the landing calculators or a phone conversation.

## 11. Seed data policy — multi-client

This plugin is licensed to multiple clients. **Seeding an editable default is not hardcoding** — v2.2.0 already ships 220/250 as Construction Settings defaults. The test is not "is it a default?" but:

> **Would this value be wrong for the next licensee?**

| category | ships as | rows |
|---|---|---|
| **Facts about the world** — jurisdictional limits identical for every UK client | Seeded, editable | The four `building_regs` rows in §3.2 and all their numeric columns; the 100mm sphere rule; 2R+G 550–700 |
| **Facts about this client** — their products, prices, suppliers, contact details | **Empty. Zero rows.** | Open-riser board rows (§4.1); front-end contact block (§7.4) |
| **Supplier specs presented as constants** | **Empty / neutral default** | `top_lip_mm` → 0, not 43 (§3.6) |

The third row is the trap. "Stairparts say this never changes" is a statement about one supplier, not about staircases. Anything in that category defaults to the neutral value and is entered as configuration.

**Nothing here blocks implementation.** Missing client values produce a clean unavailable state, not a placeholder. Cameron's inputs are data entry after the fact:

- Names and prices for the open-riser board rows
- 43mm into `top_lip_mm`
- Final customer-facing wording for the two commercial regime options and their `description` text (§3.2)

### 11.1 Follow-up (not this release)

There is no mechanism telling an admin which client-specific values are still unconfigured. As the licensee count grows this becomes a support cost. A "setup incomplete" admin notice listing empty required configuration — open-riser boards, top lip, contact block, regime descriptions — is worth a small release of its own. **Parked as a named follow-up rather than bolted onto this one.**
